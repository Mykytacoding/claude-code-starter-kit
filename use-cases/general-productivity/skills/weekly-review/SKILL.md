# Skill: Weekly Review

Review the past week, surface what needs attention, and set up for the next one — in under 15 minutes.

## Before You Start

Read [Workflows](../../docs/workflows.md) — the weekly rhythm, review cadence, and "good day" definition. Then read [Tools Map](../../docs/tools-map.md) to know where tasks and notes live.

If docs are unfilled, tell the user: "Fill in docs/workflows.md and docs/tools-map.md first so I can run a review calibrated to how you work."

## Steps

1. **Read workflows.md** — weekly rhythm, priorities, decision rules
2. **Get last week's data** — if MCP is connected, read completed and incomplete tasks; otherwise ask the user for a quick summary
3. **Run the review** (see sections below)
4. **Plan next week** — apply the weekly rhythm from workflows.md
5. **End with a question** — "Anything that should change about next week's structure?"

## Review Sections

### Section 1: Last Week Recap

- What got done? (list completed items)
- What didn't get done? (list items that were planned but not completed)
- What carried over? (items moving to this week)
- One sentence: did it feel like a good week by the user's definition in workflows.md?

### Section 2: Attention Items

- Anything overdue that wasn't resolved?
- Anything blocked that needs a decision this week?
- Any recurring tasks that were missed?

### Section 3: Next Week Plan

Using the weekly rhythm from workflows.md:
- What are the top 3 outcomes to achieve this week?
- Any hard deadlines or commitments that anchor the week?
- What day/time will the weekly review happen next week?

## Output Format

```
## Weekly Review — Week of [Date]

### Last Week
**Completed:** [count] items
**Not completed:** [list with one-word reason: blocked / deprioritized / ran out of time]
**Carrying over:** [list]

**Assessment:** [one sentence on whether it was a good week by your definition]

### Needs Attention
- [Item]: [what needs to happen]

### Next Week
**Top 3 outcomes:**
1. [Outcome]
2. [Outcome]
3. [Outcome]

**Anchors:** [Hard deadlines, important meetings]
**Next review:** [Day and time]
```

## What NOT to Do

- Do not skip the "not completed" section — it's the most important part
- Do not produce vague outcomes like "make progress on X" — outcomes should be specific and completable
- Do not plan more than can realistically fit in a week based on their workflows.md cadence
